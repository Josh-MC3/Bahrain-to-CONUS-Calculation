02 — the three rules that decide everything

Why the math moves the way it does

CZTE

One day physically in a combat zone makes the entire month of base pay federal-tax-free. Leave the zone for a full calendar month, and that month is taxed normally.

BAH

BAH follows your orders, not your body. On TAD to Jacksonville, you keep drawing Bahrain-rate BAH the whole time — it only changes once you're on leave at a different address.

BAS

BAS is never taxed — in the zone or out of it. Same for BAH. Only base pay's tax status changes when you leave Bahrain.

05 — sourcing & methodology

Where every number comes from

  • Base pay, BAH tables — 2026 DFAS basic pay table and BAH-without-dependents table, matched against the member's actual May 2026 LES.
  • CZTE rule — DoD FMR Vol. 7A, Ch. 44; IRS Publication 3.
  • Per diem (Jacksonville) — GSA FY2026 standard CONUS M&IE rate, $68/day (no NSA rate specified for Jacksonville).
  • Federal tax estimate — 2026 single-filer brackets and standard deduction, IRS Rev. Proc. 2025-32. Effective rate (~12.4%) applied to base pay only, since BAS and BAH are never federally taxable.
  • Leave sellback rate — DoD formula: annual base pay ÷ 360. Lifetime sellback cap is 60 days — there is no special tax-free combat-zone sellback provision; sold leave is taxable income regardless of where it was earned.
  • State tax — Washington is the member's state of residency; Washington has no state income tax. This applies regardless of physical location during leave.

This is a planning estimate built from publicly available pay tables and one individual's LES. It is not a substitute for a conversation with your finance office or admin about your specific leave balance, orders, and entitlement codes.
